{\LARGE\bfseries Modes of Argument}\par\smallskip
\noindent\rule{\linewidth}{0.4pt}\par\medskip
\noindent Each proof in these notes is tagged, in its caption, by its dominant \emph{mode of
argument}---the kind of reasoning it turns on. The letter \emph{A} abbreviates \emph{argument};
a proof that blends two is written, e.g., \textsf{[A2$+$A4]}. The tags are keyed below, and will
eventually link to a chapter on proof-writing.\par\medskip
\noindent\begin{tabular}{@{}l@{\hspace{0.9em}}p{0.78\linewidth}@{}}
\textbf{A1}  & $\eps$-of-room (forcing): a nonnegative quantity below every $\eps>0$ must be $0$.\\[3pt]
\textbf{A2}  & $\eps$--$N$: convergence of a sequence---given $\eps$, produce a threshold $N$.\\[3pt]
\textbf{A3}  & $\eps$--$\delta$: limits and continuity of functions.\\[3pt]
\textbf{A4}  & Triangle inequality, with an added-and-subtracted middle term.\\[3pt]
\textbf{A5}  & Supremum / infimum (least-upper-bound) argument.\\[3pt]
\textbf{A6}  & Open cover $\to$ finite subcover (compactness).\\[3pt]
\textbf{A7}  & Bisection / nested intervals closing on a point, via completeness.\\[3pt]
\textbf{A8}  & Subsequence extraction and diagonalisation.\\[3pt]
\textbf{A9}  & Contradiction, or the contrapositive.\\[3pt]
\textbf{A10} & Induction.\\[3pt]
\textbf{A11} & Negating a quantifier to unfold the failure of a definition.\\[3pt]
\textbf{A12} & Cases, or reduction ``without loss of generality.''\\[3pt]
\textbf{Constr} & Explicit construction of the required object.\\[3pt]
\textbf{Direct} & A direct chain of implications, with no special device.\\
\end{tabular}
